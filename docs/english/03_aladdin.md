## 7. Vocabulary & Key Phrases

- **To summon** — To order someone to be present. Used for paranormal beings (ghosts, demons, spirits) or for ordering someone to appear in court.
  - *Example: Inform Tywin Lannister that he has been summoned to court.*

- **Oath** — A formal and very serious promise.
  - *Example: Once you've sworn an oath to the Dutchman, there's no way out.*

- **To stand by** — To keep your promise; to remain loyal to something you've said or committed to.
  - *Example: I promise to accompany you, and I stand by that promise.*

- **On the outside / On the inside** — "On the outside" refers to how a person looks (appearance). "On the inside" refers to that person's character, values, and personality.
  - *Example: I'm just pointing out that you're dark on the inside and I'm dark on the outside.*

- **Showtime** — Literally: the time for a play or performance to begin. Figuratively: something exciting is about to happen.
  - *Example: The genie shouts "Showtime!" when Aladdin is about to approach the princess.*

- **In charge** — Being the boss; having control or authority over a situation.
  - *Example: You're not in charge — it's my idea, I'm in charge.*

- **Took my breath away** — Something is so extremely beautiful or surprising that you are momentarily speechless.
  - *Example: Did I take your breath away? — Absolutely.*
  - **Adjective form:** breathtaking — *The views are breathtaking.*

- **To relate to (someone/something)** — To understand someone's problems or situation because you've felt the same way. Not to be confused with "related to" (being family).
  - *Example: I can relate to being a single parent — I have a dog.*

- **Twist** — A slight modification that gives something a new look or feel. In movies, a sudden unexpected change in the story.
  - *Example: Will Smith gave his own personal twist to the character.*

- **Unique** — Unusually good and special; one of a kind.
  - *Example: What Naomi has done with Jasmine is powerful and really unique.*

- **Empowerment** — The process of giving someone more power or control over their own life or situation.
  - *Example: She brings that sense of empowerment to the character.*

- **To empower** — To give someone the power to do something; to give more control over their own life.
  - *Example: Women need to become more empowered.*

- **Incarnation** — The embodiment or physical realization of a spirit, deity, or character.
  - *Example: It's a refreshing incarnation of the character.*

- **Refreshing** — New in a very positive way; bringing a pleasing change.
  - *Example: It's refreshing talking to someone like you for a change.*

- **Rather than** — Used to indicate that something is not true and something else is; expresses preference.
  - *Example: He understood that sometimes we must work with our rivals rather than destroy them.*

- **To speak out** — To give your opinion publicly, especially to protest against or defend something.
  - *Example: Activists speak out against injustice.*

- **Destined to be** — Very likely to happen to someone in the future; fated.
  - *Example: He was always destined to be the star that he is today.*

- **Fairy tale** — A story for children about magical creatures (like Little Red Riding Hood, Cinderella, or Aladdin).
  - *Example: One of the things I love about Aladdin is that it's a fairy tale story in another part of the world.*

- **To shoot (a movie)** — To film or photograph.
  - *Example: The Lord of the Rings was shot in New Zealand.*

- **In trouble** — To be in a problematic or difficult situation.
  - *Example: He's in trouble!*

- **To get caught** — For someone (especially an authority figure) to see you breaking a rule or law. Also: to become a prisoner or unable to escape.
  - *Example: His parents caught him smoking.*

- **To step onto** — For your feet to touch the ground or floor of a particular location (implies arriving somewhere for the first time).
  - *Example: Today was the first time I actually stepped onto the field.*

- **Insane** — Literally: crazy. Slang: something unbelievable or extreme.
  - *Example: Six hundred dollars? That's insane!*
  - **Adverb form:** insanely — used to intensify adjectives. *They were insanely romantic.*

- **Set** — The place where a movie is filmed; the constructed environment for scenes.
  - *Example: Stepping onto set the first time — it was insane.*

- **Genuinely** — Honestly; in a real, sincere way. Can also be used to emphasize something (like "really" but stronger).
  - *Example: You could genuinely get lost in the streets.*

- **Stunning** — Very impressive or beautiful. Also: extremely attractive (for people's appearance or clothes).
  - *Example: Monica, you look stunning!*

- **Unlike anything I've ever seen** — A collocation used to describe something extremely impressive and completely new.
  - *Example: From the costumes to the sets, it was unlike anything I've ever seen.*

- **To double down** — To increase your efforts or determination to achieve something.
  - *Example: I need to double down and focus on studying for tomorrow's exam.*

- **To get it right** — To do something well; to achieve the desired result.
  - *Example: Everyone really doubled and tripled down on getting it right.*

- **Terrified** — Extremely scared or frightened.
  - *Example: I was terrified getting that first call.*

- **Performer** — A person who entertains people by acting, singing, dancing, or playing music. Broader than just "actor."
  - *Example: As a performer, you want to go and do things.*

- **To go into (something)** — To get involved in something; to enter a field or activity.
  - *Example: I went into music when I realized that an office job made me miserable.*

- **Iconic** — Excellent and admired by many people; widely recognized as a symbol.
  - *Example: Robin Williams gave an iconic performance as the Genie.*

- **There's no topping (something)** — It's impossible to do better than something. "To top" means to surpass or exceed.
  - *Example: Michael Phelps won fifteen gold medals — I wonder if anyone will ever top that.*

- **Room for improvement** — Space or opportunity to make something better; indicating something is not yet perfect.
  - *Example: Her English grades aren't bad, but there's still room for improvement.*

- **To take it up a notch** — To make something that's already good even better; to increase intensity or quality.
  - *Example: What do you say we crank it up a notch?*

- **Live-action** — A movie with real actors, as opposed to a cartoon or animated movie.
  - *Example: This live-action Aladdin took my breath away.*

- **Aspirations** — Strong hopes or ambitions for the future.
  - *Example: She has big aspirations and sees a greater future for the kingdom.*

- **Progression** — The process of developing or moving forward gradually.
  - *Example: The story is a progression of how she finally speaks out.*

- **Crick in the neck** — A pain or stiffness in the neck, usually from sleeping in an awkward position.
  - *Example: I woke up with a terrible crick in the neck.*

- **Possessor** — A person who owns or has something.
  - *Example: The genie must grant the possessor of the lamp three wishes.*

- **Villain** — A bad or evil character in a story, movie, or play.
  - *Example: The lamp could fall into the hands of a villain.*

- **Enthusiasm** — Intense and eager enjoyment or interest.
  - *Example: Aladdin does the total opposite of showing that level of enthusiasm.*

- **Magical** — Having the quality of magic; wonderful, enchanting.
  - *Example: It really was magical.*

- **Confess** — To admit something, especially something you feel guilty or embarrassed about. Also: to declare love.
  - *Example: The genie expects Aladdin to confess his love for the princess.*

- **Hypothetically** — In an imagined or theoretical situation, not real.
  - *Example: Totally hypothetically — do you like movies with a shocking twist?*

## 8. Useful Expressions & Collocations

- **"From... to..."** — Used to list different things that all contribute to making something special.
  - *Example: From the service to the food, and from the attention to detail to the decoration — this is the best restaurant I've ever been to.*

- **"Knows her own mind"** — Describes someone who is confident in their opinions and decisions.
  - *Example: I always remember Jasmine as strong and knows her own mind.*

- **"A ton of"** — A lot of; a large amount (informal).
  - *Example: You're going to get a ton of vocabulary.*

- **"Head over to"** — To go to a place (informal).
  - *Example: Now let's head over to the wonderful land of Aladdin.*

- **"Put to use"** — To apply or utilize something you've learned.
  - *Example: Now it's time to put to use what you learned.*

- **"Go beyond the classroom"** — To take learning outside of formal study and apply it in real life.
  - *Example: It's time to go beyond the classroom and live your English.*

## 9. Grammar — "Rather Than" Construction

We use **"rather than"** to indicate that something is NOT true/chosen, and something else IS:

**Structure:** subject + verb + **rather than** + verb/alternative

**Examples:**

- **Rather than say that she wanted to leave, she shows the qualities of a leader.**
  - (She didn't say she wanted to leave — instead, she showed leadership.)

- **I'll choose to listen to your actions rather than your words.**
  - (I won't listen to your words — I'll focus on your actions.)

- **He understood that sometimes we must work with our rivals rather than destroy them.**
  - (Not destroying them — working with them instead.)

**Also used to express preference:**
- *I'd rather stay home than go out.* (I prefer staying home.)

## 10. Cultural & Thematic Notes

### 10.1 Live-Action Remakes

- Disney's Aladdin (2019) is a live-action remake of the 1992 animated classic.
- "Live-action" means using real actors instead of animation.
- This is part of Disney's broader strategy of remaking animated classics (The Lion King, The Jungle Book, Beauty and the Beast).

---

### 10.2 Empowerment & Princess Jasmine

- In the 2019 version, Jasmine is reimagined with a stronger, more empowered role.
- She "speaks out" against injustice — particularly the idea that a woman can't rule the city.
- Will Smith describes Naomi Scott's performance as bringing "a sense of empowerment" to the character.
- The story follows Jasmine's "progression" from being held back to becoming "the leader she's destined to be."

---

### 10.3 Robin Williams' Iconic Legacy

- Robin Williams voiced the Genie in the 1992 animated Aladdin and gave what is widely considered an "iconic" performance.
- Will Smith describes being "terrified" of the role because Williams' performance was so good — "there's no topping Robin."
- Jimmy Kimmel notes Williams "didn't leave a lot of room to take it up a notch."
- This highlights a common challenge in remakes: living up to beloved original performances.

---

### 10.4 Cultural Representation

- The movie was shot in Wadi Rum, Jordan, to add authenticity to the fictional city of Agrabah.
- The production used cultural advisors to represent Arab culture as closely as possible.
- Mena Massoud describes how "you can just feel the history in the sand," emphasizing the importance of real locations over CGI.
- The attention to detail — "from the costumes to the sets" — shows a commitment to cultural accuracy.
