## 1. Scene 1 — Olaf's "In Summer" Song

- **Olaf:** Oh, I don't know why, but I've always loved the idea of summer, and sun, and all things hot!
- **Kristoff:** Really? I'm guessing you don't have much experience with heat.
- **Olaf:** Nope! But sometimes I like to close my eyes and imagine what it'd be like when summer does come.
- **Olaf:** ♪ Bees'll buzz, kids'll blow dandelion fuzz, and I'll be doing whatever snow does in summer. ♪
- **Olaf:** ♪ A drink in my hand, my snow up against the burning sand, probably getting gorgeously tanned in summer. ♪
- **Olaf:** ♪ I'll finally see a summer breeze blow away a winter storm, and find out what happens to solid water when it gets warm! ♪
- **Olaf:** ♪ And I can't wait to see what my buddies all think of me! Just imagine how much cooler I'll be in summer! ♪
- **Olaf:** ♪ The hot and the cold are both so intense — put 'em together, it just makes sense! ♪
- **Olaf:** ♪ Winter's a good time to stay in and cuddle, but put me in summer and I'll be a happy snowman! ♪
- **Olaf:** ♪ When life gets rough, I like to hold on to my dream — relaxing in the summer sun, just letting off steam. ♪
- **Olaf:** ♪ Oh the sky will be blue, and you guys'll be there too, when I finally do what frozen things do in summer! ♪
- **Anna:** I'm gonna tell him.
- **Kristoff:** Don't you dare.

## 2. Scene 2 — Olaf Reflects on Life (Frozen 2)

- **Anna:** Enjoying your new permafrost, Olaf?
- **Olaf:** A lot! I'm just living the dream, Anna. Oh, how I wish this could last forever.
- **Olaf:** And yet, change mocks us with her beauty.
- **Anna:** What's that?
- **Olaf:** Forgive me — maturity is making me poetic.
- **Olaf:** Tell me, you're older and thus all-knowing — do you ever worry about the notion that nothing is permanent?
- **Anna:** No.
- **Olaf:** I can't wait until I'm aged like you, so I don't have to worry about important things.

## 3. Vocabulary & Key Phrases

- **Love the idea of (something)** — To imagine that you would like something, even though you haven't actually experienced it.
  - *Example: I love the idea of living in Paris — sitting in a cafe every morning with a coffee and a croissant.*

- **All things (something)** — Everything related to a particular topic.
  - *Example: This guy consumes all things baseball — he's really obsessed with it.*

- **To melt** — For a solid (ice, snow) to become liquid when it gets warm.
  - *Example: As everyone but Olaf knows, snow and ice melt in summer.*

- **To buzz** — To make a continuous low-volume sound, like insects do when flying.
  - *Example: The bees were buzzing around the garden.*

- **To blow** — To create a flow of air. People blow candles out on birthday cakes.
  - *Example: Kids'll blow dandelion fuzz.*

- **Fuzz** — A fluffy mass of hair or fiber.
  - *Example: The dandelion fuzz floated away in the wind.*

- **Dandelion** — A type of flower whose seeds are easily blown into the wind. It's believed you can make a wish when you blow a dandelion.
  - *Example: She picked up a dandelion and made a wish.*

- **Up against** — Putting your weight on something; leaning on or touching something.
  - *Example: He has his back up against the wall.*

- **Tan / Tanned** — A light brown color; skin becoming bronzed in the sun (both noun and verb).
  - *Example: Look how tan she is — she got a nice tan during her trip to Mexico.*

- **Gorgeous** — Beautiful or extremely attractive. **Gorgeously** = beautifully, in a beautiful way.
  - *Example: Probably getting gorgeously tanned in summer.*

- **Buddy** — An informal way to refer to a close friend. Similar words: **pal**, **mate**, **bro**.
  - *Example: Hey Mowgli, my friend, my buddy, my pal!*

- **Cool** — Two meanings: (1) calm and in control, or slang for fun/impressive/in style; (2) slightly cold in a pleasant way.
  - *Example: You like Sam — he's a very cool guy.*

- **Intense** — Very strong, extreme, or powerful in degree.
  - *Example: The hot and the cold are both so intense.*

- **To stay in** — To decide to stay inside or at home instead of going out.
  - *Example: We went to the movie theater last night, but tonight we're staying in.*

- **To cuddle** — To put your arms around somebody and hold them in a loving way.
  - *Example: Winter's a good time to stay in and cuddle.*

- **Rough** — Hard or difficult (in this context).
  - *Example: When life gets rough, I like to hold on to my dream.*

- **To hold on to** — To hold something tightly so you don't drop it. Figuratively: to keep something (a dream, a memory) alive.
  - *Example: Hold on to your seat — we're going off-road!*
  - *Figurative: Hold on to that map — you will need it.*

- **Don't you dare** — A strong warning telling someone not to do something; expresses anger.
  - *Example: Don't you dare use science against me!*

- **To dare** — To have the courage to do something. Also: to challenge or provoke someone to try something.
  - *Example: I dare you to jump off that cliff!*

- **Permafrost** — An area of land that is permanently frozen due to extreme low temperatures. In Frozen 2, Olaf is made of permanent snow by Elsa's magic.
  - *Example: The Arctic tundra is covered in permafrost.*

- **To live the dream** — To have the lifestyle you always wanted, especially after becoming successful.
  - *Example: I always wanted to be a famous singer, and now I'm living the dream.*

- **To last** — To continue for a period of time; to continue to exist.
  - *Example: I haven't been in a relationship that lasted longer than a month.*

- **Yet** — In this context: despite that; used to add something surprising after what was just said.
  - *Example: And yet, change mocks us with her beauty.*

- **To mock** — To make fun of someone; to ridicule or treat with contempt.
  - *Example: Olaf says change mocks us — it makes humans feel insignificant.*

- **Maturity** — The state of becoming older and having more life experience.
  - *Example: Forgive me — maturity is making me poetic.*

- **Poetic** — Expressing emotions and ideas as if from a poem; beautifully expressive.
  - *Example: Maturity is making me poetic.*

- **What's that?** — An expression used when you don't understand or didn't hear what someone said. Means "What did you say?" or "Repeat that."
  - *Example: "Change mocks us with her beauty." — "What's that?"*

- **Thus** — An adverb meaning consequently or therefore.
  - *Example: Barcelona beat Real Madrid in the finals, thus becoming champion.*

- **All-knowing** — An adjective meaning knowing everything; aware of everything.
  - *Example: Most religions describe God as all-knowing.*

- **Notion** — An idea, belief, or opinion about something.
  - *Example: Do you ever worry about the notion that nothing is permanent?*

- **Permanent** — Lasting forever; not changing or temporary.
  - *Example: Do you ever worry about the notion that nothing is permanent?*

- **Do you ever** — Simply means "do you normally do something?"
  - *Example: Do you ever go to the gym?*

- **Aged** — Very old; having lived for a long time.
  - *Example: I can't wait until I'm aged like you.*

- **Nope** — An informal way to say "no."
  - *Example: Do you have experience with heat? — Nope!*

- **Solid** — Firm and hard; not liquid or gas. "Solid water" = ice or snow.
  - *Example: Find out what happens to solid water when it gets warm.*

- **To imagine** — To form a picture or idea in your mind of something you haven't experienced.
  - *Example: I like to close my eyes and imagine what it'd be like.*

## 4. Weather, Seasons & Nature Vocabulary

- **Summer** — The warmest season of the year, between spring and autumn.
  - *Example: I've always loved the idea of summer.*

- **Winter** — The coldest season of the year, between autumn and spring.
  - *Example: Winter's a good time to stay in and cuddle.*

- **Breeze** — A light, cool wind.
  - *Example: One of the nicest things about being at the beach is feeling the gentle breeze off the water.*

- **Storm** — A violent disturbance of the atmosphere with strong winds, rain, snow, or thunder.
  - *Example: I'll finally see a summer breeze blow away a winter storm.*

- **Snowstorm** — A heavy fall of snow with strong winds.
  - *Example: The snowstorm made the roads impossible to drive on.*

- **Heat** — The quality of being hot; high temperature.
  - *Example: I'm guessing you don't have much experience with heat.*

- **Sand** — The fine, loose grains found on beaches and deserts.
  - *Example: My snow up against the burning sand.*

- **Burning** — Extremely hot (when describing temperature).
  - *Example: The burning sand was too hot to walk on barefoot.*

- **Steam** — Water in the form of gas, normally produced when water is heated.
  - *Example: The hot soup was letting off steam.*

- **Puddle** — A small pool of water, especially rainwater, on the ground.
  - *Example: A snowman in summer would become a puddle.*

## 5. Idioms & Expressions

- **Can't wait (to do something)** — To be very excited about something or anxious to do it.
  - *Example: I can't wait to see you! I've missed you too.*

- **To let off steam / blow off steam** — To do or say something that helps you get rid of strong or tense feelings or energy.
  - *Example: She jogs after work to let off steam.*
  - *Example: We've been working so hard this semester — we really need to blow off some steam.*

- **Makes sense** — Is logical; is easy to understand or agree with.
  - *Example: Put 'em together — it just makes sense!*

## 6. Connected Speech — Reducing "Them" to "'em"

In casual speech, native speakers commonly reduce **"them"** to just **"'em"** /əm/:

- **put them** → **put 'em** /pʊɾəm/
- **help them** → **help 'em** /hɛlpəm/

**Important notes:**
- The correct reduced pronunciation is **'em** /əm/, NOT "aim"
- Natives almost always connect the verb with **'em** as one continuous sound
- When a T comes between two vowel sounds (American English), it sounds like a D:
  - **put 'em** → sounds like /pʊɾəm/ (tap T)

**Examples from Friends:**
- "You have to put them together yourself" → "put 'em together"
- "I say we just put them all together" → "put 'em all together"

## 7. Grammar — "Do/Does" for Emphatic Affirmation

Normally, **do/does** are auxiliary verbs used in questions and negative statements:
- *Do you like it?*
- *I do not like it.*

But they can also be used in **affirmative sentences for emphasis** — to express strong feelings:

- **"He thinks I don't love him, but I do love him."** — Emphasizes that you really do love him.
- **"When summer does come"** — Olaf adds emphasis; could be replaced by "finally": *"when summer finally comes."*

**More examples:**
- *I do see something — yes, I do!*
- *She does know what she's talking about.*

## 8. Cultural & Thematic Notes

### 8.1 Olaf's Running Joke — Snow in Summer

- Olaf is a magical snowman who loves summer but doesn't understand that snow melts in warm weather.
- The entire "In Summer" song is built on this irony — everything Olaf dreams of doing would actually destroy him.
- This creates both humor for adults (who understand the irony) and charm for children (who enjoy Olaf's enthusiasm).

---

### 8.2 The Puddle Rhyme

- One of the cleverest jokes in the song is the near-rhyme with "puddle":
  - *"Winter's a good time to stay in and cuddle, but put me in summer and I'll be a..."* — the expected rhyme is **puddle** (what a snowman would become).
  - But Olaf says **"happy snowman"** instead, showing he's completely unaware of what would actually happen.

---

### 8.3 Double Meanings — "Cooler" and "Letting Off Steam"

- **"Just imagine how much cooler I'll be"** — Olaf means "more impressive/stylish," but ironically, he'd actually get warmer (not cooler) and melt.
- **"Just letting off steam"** — The idiom means releasing tension, but literally, hot snow would literally let off steam (water turning to gas). This is another example of the movie using wordplay with Olaf's snow nature.

---

### 8.4 Olaf's Poetic Maturity in Frozen 2

- In Frozen 2, Olaf has grown more philosophical and reflective.
- He says "maturity is making me poetic" and asks deep questions about permanence and change.
- His line "change mocks us with her beauty" is surprisingly profound — acknowledging that change in nature is beautiful but also makes us feel powerless.
- This character development contrasts with his naive, carefree personality in the first film.
