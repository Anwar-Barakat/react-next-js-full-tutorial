## Tripz

## 6. What is Tripz Project?

Tripz is an online platform that helps schools organize educational trips easily.

**The problem:**
Schools face many problems when booking trips. For example, if a school wants to visit Legoland, they usually must contact a mediator/middleman. This mediator charges extra money and often responds late because they are busy. Most schools depend on this method.

Another option is to apply directly on the destination's website, but the response can take one or two weeks. Even after getting a reply, the suggested date may not suit the school. Sometimes the destination asks the school to wait because they are coordinating with other schools, or they ask the school to choose a different date. This process is slow, boring, and not modern.

**The solution:**
Tripz solves these problems. Schools simply choose a destination, enter details like number of students, supervisors, grades, sections, and select a date. The booking is completed in less than one minute.

**It connects:**
- Schools
- Educational destinations
- Parents
- Tripz administrators

**In short:** Tripz allows schools to book trips, parents to pay securely, destinations to manage tickets and events, and admins to monitor everything in one system.

---

## 7. How does Tripz Work?

### For Schools
- The school logs into its dashboard.
- It browses destinations and tickets.
- The school selects:
  - Number of students and supervisors
  - Grades and sections
  - Trip date (and shift if available)
- A booking is created with status **Pending**.
- The destination reviews and approves the booking.
- After approval:
  - The school adds extra fees (e.g. bus fees).
  - A payment link is generated and sent to parents.
- Schools can:
  - Track student registrations
  - Export lists (PDF / Excel)
  - Monitor payments in real time

### For Parents
- Parents receive a secure link from the school.
- They register and add their children.
- They view full trip details.
- They pay online easily.
- The school receives instant payment notifications.

### For Destinations
- Approve or reject trip bookings.
- Define:
  - Tickets
  - Add-ons
  - Shifts
  - Blocked days (holidays, maintenance)
  - Review release-payment requests

### Payments
- Money is released only if conditions are met:
  - Minimum students reached
  - Dates not exceeded
- When released, funds are automatically split into:
  - Destination share
  - Tripz platform fee
- Refunds are supported if needed.

---

## 8. What is the Importance of Tripz?

Tripz is important because it:
- Digitizes a complex manual process.
- Reduces coordination problems between schools and destinations.
- Provides secure and transparent payments.
- Prevents miscommunication using real-time chat and notifications.
- Ensures accountability with admin monitoring.
- Saves time for schools, parents, and destinations.
- Supports large-scale operations with many users and roles.

**In short:** Tripz makes educational trips organized, safe, and scalable.

## 9. What was Your Role in Tripz?

- My role in Tripz was a **Full-Stack Developer**.
- I worked on building and maintaining the platform.
- Using Laravel for the backend and Inertia with React for the frontend.

**My responsibilities included:**
- Implementing authentication and authorization.
- Building a complex role-based access control (RBAC) system.
- Developing school, destination, parent, and admin dashboards.
- Designing booking workflows with multiple states.
- Integrating Stripe for secure payments and automated fund splitting.
- Building real-time notifications and chat features.
- Creating data exports (PDF and Excel).
- Monitoring analytics using admin charts and dashboards.

**Vue to React migration:**
One of the biggest challenges was converting the entire frontend from Vue to React using Inertia. This migration was completed in a short time while keeping the system stable and scalable.

**Why React was chosen:**
- It handles complex state and workflows better.
- Strong TypeScript support.
- Easier scaling for large UI systems.
- It has a very large community and many supported libraries.

**How the decision was made:**
- During the planning phase, the project lead asked which frontend framework was better.
- I recommended React because it is very popular and works well with modern UI libraries.
- A senior team member initially preferred Vue because it integrates well with Laravel.
- After researching further, the project lead agreed that React is stronger.
- He agreed with my recommendation and decided to use React.

## 39. What do you think about using AI in writing code?

AI is no longer optional in software development — it is becoming a core part of how modern companies build products.

**Why AI matters in coding:**
- It increases **productivity** significantly — developers can build features faster.
- It helps write **cleaner, more consistent code**, especially when the team follows a unified structure.
- It **reduces development time**, allowing teams to ship more features in less time.
- It assists with **code reviews, debugging, refactoring**, and generating boilerplate code.
- It helps maintain **best practices** across the entire codebase.

**Real-world examples:**
- **Block (formerly Square)** laid off around 4,000 employees and replaced many roles with AI agents. Their CEO said AI can now handle work that previously required large teams.
- **Klarna** reduced their workforce by 700 employees, with AI doing the work of what used to require hundreds of customer service agents.
- **Many companies** are now building entire workflows using AI agents — not just for coding, but for testing, deployment, and documentation.

**AI tools developers use today:**
- **Claude Code / Claude** — AI assistant for writing, reviewing, and debugging code.
- **GitHub Copilot** — AI pair programmer integrated into the editor.
- **Cursor** — AI-first code editor built for working with AI agents.
- **ChatGPT** — General-purpose AI for problem-solving and code generation.
- **v0 by Vercel** — AI that generates UI components from descriptions.

**My perspective:**
- AI does not replace developers — it **amplifies** them.
- A developer who uses AI effectively can do the work of 2-3 developers.
- The key is knowing **how to prompt, review, and guide** AI output — not blindly accepting it.
- Developers who resist AI will fall behind. Those who embrace it will be far more productive.
- It is essential to **stay up to date** with AI tools and workflows because the industry is moving fast.

**In short:** AI is transforming how software is built. Companies are investing heavily in AI-powered development. As a developer, using AI is not a shortcut — it is a skill that makes you more productive, helps you write better code, and keeps you competitive in the job market.

## 40. Do you know Node.js or Express?

Yes, I have knowledge of Node.js and Express.

**What is Node.js?**
- Node.js is a **JavaScript runtime** built on Chrome's V8 engine.
- It allows you to run JavaScript **outside the browser** — on the server side.
- It uses an **event-driven, non-blocking I/O model**, which makes it fast and efficient.
- It is great for building **APIs, real-time apps, and microservices**.

**What is Express?**
- Express is a **minimal and flexible framework** for Node.js.
- It simplifies building web servers and APIs.
- It handles **routing, middleware, request/response** processing.
- Think of it like Laravel for PHP — but lighter and more flexible.

**Basic concepts I understand:**

```javascript
// Creating a simple Express server
const express = require('express');
const app = express();

// Middleware - runs on every request
app.use(express.json());

// Routes
app.get('/api/users', (req, res) => {
  res.json({ users: [] });
});

app.post('/api/users', (req, res) => {
  const { name, email } = req.body;
  // Create user logic
  res.status(201).json({ name, email });
});

// Start server
app.listen(3000, () => {
  console.log('Server running on port 3000');
});
```

**Key Node.js/Express concepts:**
- **Middleware** — functions that run between request and response (like Laravel middleware).
- **Routing** — defining endpoints for GET, POST, PUT, DELETE.
- **req/res** — request and response objects (similar to Laravel's Request/Response).
- **npm** — package manager for installing dependencies (like Composer in PHP).
- **Async/Await** — handling asynchronous operations (database queries, API calls).
- **REST API** — building RESTful endpoints, same principles as Laravel API.

**How it compares to what I already know:**

- **Routing** — Laravel: `Route::get()` → Express: `app.get()`
- **Middleware** — Laravel: `$this->middleware()` → Express: `app.use()`
- **Request data** — Laravel: `$request->input()` → Express: `req.body`
- **Response** — Laravel: `response()->json()` → Express: `res.json()`
- **ORM** — Laravel: Eloquent → Express: Prisma / Sequelize
- **Package manager** — Laravel: Composer → Express: npm / yarn / pnpm
- **Validation** — Laravel: Form Request → Express: Joi / Zod / express-validator

**My perspective on learning new technologies:**
- Nowadays, learning new technologies is **essential** and **not hard** — especially with AI.
- The **core concepts** are the same across frameworks: routing, middleware, authentication, database, API design.
- If you understand the fundamentals well, switching from Laravel to Express (or any framework) is about learning **syntax, not concepts**.
- With AI tools like Claude and Copilot, I can quickly understand new frameworks, generate code, and build features — what used to take weeks now takes days.
- The most important skill is **the ability to learn fast**, not memorizing one specific framework.

**In short:** Yes, I know Node.js and Express. My main experience is with Laravel, but the backend concepts are transferable. And with AI tools available today, picking up any new technology is faster than ever. What matters is understanding the fundamentals — the rest is syntax.

## 41. Useful Interview Phrases (When You Need a Moment)

### When you don't understand the question

- "Could you please repeat the question? I want to make sure I understand it correctly."
- "Could you rephrase that, please? I want to give you the best answer."
- "Just to clarify — are you asking about [your understanding]? I want to make sure we're on the same page."
- "That's a great question. Could you give me a bit more context so I can answer it properly?"

### When you need time to think

- "That's a great question. Let me think about it for a moment."
- "Give me a second to organize my thoughts on this."
- "I want to give you a clear answer, so let me take a moment to think."

### When you don't know the answer

- "I haven't worked with that specific technology yet, but I'm familiar with the concept and I'm confident I can learn it quickly."
- "I don't have direct experience with that, but I've worked on something similar using [related technology]."
- "Honestly, I'm not sure about that specific detail, but I would research it and get back to you. I believe in being honest rather than guessing."
- "That's something I haven't encountered yet, but I'm always eager to learn new things — especially with AI tools that make learning much faster."

### When you want to redirect to your strengths

- "I haven't used that exact tool, but in my experience with [similar tool], I handled it by..."
- "That's an interesting area. In my projects, I focused more on [your strength], where I..."
- "While I haven't done exactly that, the underlying concept is similar to what I did in [project name]."

### When the interviewer mentions something you're not familiar with

- "I'm not deeply familiar with that yet, but could you tell me more about how your team uses it? I'd love to understand the context."
- "I've heard of it but haven't used it in production. I'm happy to learn more about it."

### When you want to confirm your answer

- "Does that answer your question, or would you like me to go deeper into a specific part?"
- "I hope that gives you a clear picture. Let me know if you'd like more details."

### General tips

- **Never lie.** If you don't know, say so honestly — interviewers respect honesty over fake confidence.
- **Always connect back** to what you do know — show you can learn and adapt.
- **Stay calm.** Taking a moment to think shows confidence, not weakness.
- **Smile and stay positive.** Your attitude matters as much as your technical skills.
- **It's okay to ask for clarification.** Good communication starts with understanding the question.
